Media Constraints¶

Writes¶

Writes can be done at the granularity of a single physical page. In the example geometry this means a minimum of four sector addresses must be constructed, ordered from lowest to highest.

For a write to succeed then the page must reside in an erased block. This constraint is often referred to as erase-before-write. Consequentially, one cannot “update” a given flash page.

Reads¶

Reads can be done at the granularity of a single sector. In the example geometry this means a minimum of one sector address must be constructed.

For a read to success it must reside within a fully written block, sometimes also referred to as a “closed block”.
